# How To Read Results

This tool is useful at the beginning of a reporting process, not at the end of one.

## Journalist Workflow

1. Pick a party, election year, and factor.
2. Read the correlation direction and strength.
3. Inspect the high and low municipalities.
4. Check whether the pattern survives obvious alternative explanations.
5. Use public sources, local context, and reporting before writing a claim.

## Interesting But Not Final

A result is interesting when:

- the correlation is moderate or strong
- the direction is politically or geographically plausible
- the pattern appears across enough municipalities
- the outliers are explainable rather than random-looking

It is not final until the source data, local context, and alternative explanations have been checked.

## Pattern To Story

Use this order:

`pattern -> check -> context -> cautious sentence`

Do not jump from:

`pattern -> cause`

## Responsible Phrasing

- "The party tended to perform better in municipalities with higher income."
- "The relationship is descriptive and municipality-level."
- "The pattern suggests a reporting lead, not a causal conclusion."
- "The 2026 layer should be read as a bridge layer."

## Phrasing To Avoid

- "Income made voters choose the party."
- "High-income voters voted for the party."
- "The data proves why the party won."
- "This predicts the next Folketing election."

## Minimum Check Before Publishing

- Confirm the selected year and factor are available in the public layer.
- Read the methodology note.
- Check the top and bottom municipalities.
- Compare the result with at least one non-app source.
- Keep "correlation is not causation" visible in the claim.
